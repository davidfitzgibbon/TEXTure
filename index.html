Jason Santa Maria gave a great talk at Build. He talked about web typography and gave us various hints and tips. At about 17:30mins in, he gives us a tip as to how people visualize our designs. How they don't see the design and it's details like us, the designer.

He said:

Most people think that when you design something like this:

When someone is going to read it, they see the entirety of it. They see your nice type choice, they see how well defined all of your type is, the line length and the carefully considered spacing. But I think people see something a little bit more like this:

It's texture. It's content. They only notice it when something isnt right.
